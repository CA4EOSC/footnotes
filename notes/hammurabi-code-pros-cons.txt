Pros (Arguments for):

    Transparency: The code was placed in the city center "so that everyone could see." This introduced the concept of "Ignorantia juris non excusat" (ignorance of the law is no excuse) and limited the ability of judges to be arbitrary.
    Protection of the Vulnerable: The transcript explicitly mentions rules "defending widows, orphans, and tenant farmers," suggesting a genuine, if primitive, concern for social welfare and the "public order."
    Standardization: It unified a vast, diverse empire under a single legal framework, replacing tribal blood feuds with state-mediated restitution (like the "eye for an eye" principle).
